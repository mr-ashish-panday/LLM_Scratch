# Title Transformation Examples

## STEM — Biomedical Research

### Before (Weak)
> "A Study on the Effects of a New Drug on Cancer in Patients"

**Problems**: Starts with "A Study on", uses "new" (weak keyword), "cancer" too generic, "patients" unspecified.

### After — Declarative (Highest Citation Impact)
> "Ustekinumab treatment in ulcerative colitis improves clinical remission rates in a real-world nationwide registry study"
- Words: 15 | Characters: 103
- Front-loaded: drug name + condition in first 40 chars
- Active verb: "improves"
- Includes study design

### After — Descriptive (Conservative Journals)
> "Effect of Benzathine Penicillin Prophylaxis on Rheumatic Fever Recurrence in Pediatric Patients"
- Words: 13 | Characters: 93
- PICO-aligned: I (Benzathine Penicillin) → O (Recurrence) → P (Pediatric Patients)

### After — Nature-Compliant (<66 chars, no punctuation)
> "Ustekinumab improves ulcerative colitis remission rates"
- Words: 7 | Characters: 56
- Zero punctuation, single flowing declarative sentence

## STEM — Molecular Biology

### Before (Weak)
> "The Control of Organ Development in Fish"

**Problems**: Starts with "The", "organ" and "fish" too vague, no gene name, no specificity.

### After — Declarative
> "The novel gene 'exdpf' regulates pancreas development in zebrafish"
- Words: 9 | Characters: 67
- Specific gene marker, exact organ, binomial-level species

## Social Sciences — Psychology (APA Style)

### Before (Weak)
> "A Study of How Context Affects Student Performance"

**Problems**: "A Study of" filler, "how" signals interrogative energy, "student" unclear.

### After — Descriptive (APA Variable Format)
> "Effect of Context on Performance Approach Orientation"
- Words: 8 | Characters: 53
- Clean IV (Context) → DV (Performance Approach Orientation)
- No filler, no leading "The"

### After — With Population & Design
> "Effect of Classroom Context on Performance Approach Orientation in Undergraduate Students: A Longitudinal Study"
- Words: 16 | Characters: 112
- Colon structure adds design info
- Population specified

---

## Public Health — SPICED Framework

### Before (Weak)
> "A New Method for Assessing the Incidence of Gall Stones and Cancer Risk"

**Problems**: "A New Method" is filler, "incidence" and "risk" are weak keywords, "gall stones" should be more specific.

### After — Descriptive
> "Gallbladder Polyps Growth Rate as a Predictor of Neoplastic Transformation: A Prospective Cohort Study"
- Words: 15 | Characters: 101
- Specific clinical terms replace generic ones
- Design appended via colon

### After — Declarative
> "Gallbladder polyp growth rate independently predicts neoplastic transformation in asymptomatic adults"
- Words: 12 | Characters: 101
- Active verb: "predicts"
- Front-loaded specific key phrase

## Humanities — Literary Analysis

### Before (Weak)
> "Symbolism in Fitzgerald's Work"

**Problems**: Far too vague, no specific text, no interpretive angle.

### After — Colon Paradigm (Standard Humanities)
> "The Green Light and the American Mirage: Deconstructing Aspirational Symbolism in F. Scott Fitzgerald's *The Great Gatsby*"
- Words: 18 | Characters: 121
- Evocative hook before colon, specific analysis after
- Novel title italicized per MLA
- Author's full name included

## Qualitative Research — SPIDER Framework

### Before (Weak)
> "Research on Nurses' Experiences with Burnout"

**Problems**: "Research on" filler, generic population, no methodology.

### After — Descriptive
> "Lived Experiences of Burnout Among ICU Nurses: A Phenomenological Study"
- Words: 11 | Characters: 71
- SPIDER: Sample (ICU Nurses), Phenomenon (Burnout), Design (Phenomenological)
- Colon separates topic from methodology

## Keyword Metadata — The Redundancy Fix

### ❌ Bad: Redundant Keywords
**Title**: "Effect of Benzathine Penicillin on Rheumatic Fever Recurrence"
**Keywords**: Benzathine Penicillin, Rheumatic Fever, Recurrence ← ALL already in the title!

### ✅ Good: Non-Redundant Keywords
**Title**: "Effect of Benzathine Penicillin on Rheumatic Fever Recurrence"
**Keywords**: secondary prophylaxis, streptococcal pharyngitis, acute rheumatic carditis, intramuscular antibiotic injection, Jones criteria ← NONE in the title, all expand discoverability
